Dictocounter is an iPhone app that counts which words you say. To accomplish this, the app records your audio and parses it using iPhone's built-in dictation. This data is then displayed on the homepage of the app, where a user can see how many times they've said any word since downloading the app. The data are also represented visually under the graphs tab. These visaul tools can help users discover if their speech patterns follow the natural tendicies of literature (i.e. Zipf's language law).

(WIP): A third tab (profile) will showcase the most common words a user has said, as well as any temporal abnormalities in a user's speech. The latter may include words that a user has used frequently in a previous time period, but less recently, and vice versa.

The Reverse Polish Notation (RPN) Calculator is a C++ script that efficiently parses and computes an expression inputted by the user. The calculator can parse and evaluate expressions containing mathematical constants and multi-input functions. I've extended the calculator to work in the complex plane and recognize A*exp(jΦ) as a magnitude and phase. Additionally, the user can store numbers or past answers as variables, which can then be referenced in future expressions.

C++

The Markov Chain Text Generator is a text generator that parses a user-provided dataset to complete inputted sentences or paragraphs. Words are filled in character-by-character based on k (the length of the evaluating string) and each evaluating string's probability distribution, which is derived from the dataset. For example, if k = 5 and the inputted fragment is "Hello wor" then the next character will be chosen based on the learned probability distribution for the length 5 string "o wor". Most likely, the next character will be 'l' as in "Hello world", but it may also be 'k' as in "Go to work", and many other characters based on the sentences given in the dataset.
